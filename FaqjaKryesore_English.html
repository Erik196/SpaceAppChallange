What Is Our Carbon Footprint?

WHAT IS OUR CARBOON FOOTPRINT?

Your carbon footprint is the sum of all emissions of CO2 (carbon dioxide), which were induced by your activities in a given time frame. Usually a carbon footprint is calculated for the time period of a year. The total amount of greenhouse gases produced to directly and indirectly support human activities, usually expressed in equivalent tons of carbon dioxide (CO2).

Each of the following activities adds 1 kg of CO2 to your personal carbon footprint:

  1. Production of 1/3 of an American cheeseburger
  2. Production of 2 plastic bottles
  3. Production of 5 plastic bags
  4. Operate your computer for 32 hours (60 Watt consumption assumed)
  5. Fly with a plane a distance of 2.2 km or 1.375 miles.
  6. Drive with your car a distance of 6 km or 3.75 miles (assuming 7.3 liters petrol per 100 km or 39 mpg)
  7. Travel by public transportation (train or bus) a distance of 10 to 12 km (6.5 to 7 miles)

WHAT IS GHG?

Greenhouse gases are those that can absorb and emit infrared radiation. The carbon footprint is a very powerful tool to understand the impact of personal behavior on global warming. Carbon dioxide is a so called greenhouse gas causing global warming. Other greenhouse gases which might be emitted as a result of your activities are e.g. methane and ozone. These greenhouse gases are normally also taken into account for the carbon footprint. They are converted into the amount of CO2 that would cause the same effects on global warming (this is called equivalent CO2 amount).

DEPELTION OF RESOURCES

Large carbon footprints deplete resources on large and small scales, from a country's deforestation activities to one home's increased use of air conditioning. The more those with large carbon footprints use resources, the more greenhouse gases increase and spur further climate change.

CLIMATE CHANGE

Climate change is the ultimate effect of large carbon footprints. Greenhouse gases, whether natural or human-produced, contribute to the warming of the planet. From 1990 to 2005, carbon dioxide emissions increased by 31%. By 2008, the emissions had contributed to a 35% increase in radiative warming, or a shift in Earth's energy balance toward warming, over 1990 levels.

SOLUTION

As an individual, the change can start with using an environmentally-friendly transport. Using public transport these days is not hard or costly at all. Statistics say that in western Europe, it is 10 times better to use public transport than traveling by car. Also it is important to avoid flights as much as possible. The greenhouse gas balance of flights has the highest level of emissions when compared to other forms of transport.

Secondly, it is crucial to save energy. Energy takes a lot of pollution to be created, and sometimes it is produced by recourses that are not recyclable. For instance, replacing normal light bulbs with LEDs, turning the light off when leaving the room, taking short showers , switching off devices that are in standby mode, closing the windows when heating the room or drying the clothes with the energy created by the sun, a renewable type of energy. All these small things done by each of us can reduce the emissions in a considerable scale.

If we start this from ourselves, then it can become a global situation and that is precisely what it should be. As a community, there are many actions we could take too. The first is surely to raise awareness. There could be campaigns that include real scientists explaining the real cause and the real consequences that could come with climate change. If everyone would understand properly in what situation we are, it would be a lot easier to take action. After understanding the concept, we could start by the most important thing: using renewable energy in every house. Renewable energy is a very clean and cheap type of energy. For example, not warming water with electricity but with solar panels. Many things follow after this, like having car-free days, consuming less food, recycling plastic and dangerous materials for the earth, planting trees etc.

To conclude, this is a case that includes us all. As a community of states it is crucial to immediately leave the politics behind and think of our future, of our successors’ future, for our home’s future. We were provided all of this, we were provided with life, and it is our job to take care of it. Change has to come from each, and it should end with us as one.
